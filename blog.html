Designing with HCI in Mind

Posted June 20, 2025

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is more than just rules and best practices — it's about understanding how users think, behave, and feel. As a designer, I strive to create interfaces that are not just beautiful, but usable and satisfying.

My design philosophy centers around reducing cognitive load. I use whitespace strategically, keep interactions predictable, and always test for accessibility. Simplicity isn’t just aesthetics — it’s usability.

Every click, color, and flow is intentional. I want users to feel at home, empowered, and never lost. That’s the HCI spirit.

Why I Chose Graphic Design

Posted July 1, 2025

Design found me before I found it. From sketching soccer logos as a child to laying out book covers, I always gravitated toward visual expression. Graphic design became my language — a way to connect ideas and emotions.

It’s not just about visuals; it’s about purpose. Every poster I create, every layout I build, speaks to a real need or message. I’m fascinated by how color, shape, and type can influence thought and perception.

Design, for me, is storytelling — and I’m committed to crafting meaningful, intentional, and human-centered stories with every project.
